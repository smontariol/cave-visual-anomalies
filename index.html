CAVE Benchmark

Abstract

Humans can naturally identify, reason about, and explain anomalies in their environment. In computer vision, this long-standing challenge remains limited to industrial defects or synthetic anomalies, failing to capture the richness of real-world unpredictability. We introduce CAVE, the first benchmark of real-world commonsense anomalies, supporting three open-ended tasks: anomaly description, explanation, and justification. With fine-grained annotations categorizing anomalies based on their manifestation, complexity, severity, and surprisal, CAVE provides a cognitively grounded framework for evaluating VLMs. Our results show that even state-of-the-art VLMs struggle with anomaly perception and commonsense reasoning, underscoring the need for advances in visual understanding and reasoning capabilities.

Key Findings

  • State-of-the-art VLMs achieve only ~56% F1 on anomaly detection, far below human-level.
  • Models perform better on severe/surprising anomalies, but struggle with complex reasoning tasks.
  • Visual grounding and structured prompting improve recall modestly, but issues remain.
  • Cultural and commonsense gaps highlight the need for more inclusive evaluation.
